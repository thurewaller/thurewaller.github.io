Fighting Fires in Cars

Designer duo Jonas Forsman and Lars Wettre started with an idea – to create a fire extinguisher that you want to have visible in your home. Having a fire extinguisher close at hand in case of a fire could save lives. The idea resulted in the Firephant extinguisher released in 2012. The wall mounted extinguisher comes in two sizes – 1 kg and 2 kg – and was awarded Red Dot Best of the Best in 2012.

 

In 2017 Firephant wanted to extend their product range with another fire extinguisher – this time for cars. To do so they initiated this project with the aim to research and conceptualise a fire extinguisher for cars. This included finding out where and how to mount the extinguisher, how to use it and how to make it fit into Firephant’s existing product line. Apart from this it also had to be cheaper to produce than the original Firephant extinguisher.

PRE-STUDY

Getting to Know the Original

The first step was to understand what made the Firephant fire extinguisher so successful and what could be improved. To do so we disassembled it and talked to the designers and manufacturers.

PRE-STUDY

Learning From the Pros

Where do you go to learn more about fires? The fire station of course! At an early stage of the project we visited a local fire station to interview firefighters. Apart from interviews we also got to try different fire extinguishers.

 

The new fire extinguisher should be an aftermarket product and thus fit into as many cars as possible. To find common denominators between car models we visited a few car dealerships. Inside the cabins of the cars there were often tight with space and and hard to find common denominators. In the trunks of the cars almost all had the same kind of felt fabric and loops for securing the load.

IDEATION & PROTOTYPING

Testing the Ideas

During the ideation and concept development phase physical mockups played a large role. By building mockups from empty glass bottles covered in clay a realistic weight was achieved. This made it possible to test the ergonomics and the handling of the concepts. During the project the mockups were tested and evaluated with potential users.

FINAL DESIGN

Lighter and More User Friendly

The final design is a small 1 kg powder extinguisher mounted in the trunk of the car. The use of an aluminium cylinder makes the extinguisher really light, with a total weight of less than 1.4 kg. This is good both for handling of the extinguisher and for the fuel consumption of the car.

 

The extinguisher can be used with one hand thanks to the design of the plastic handle that screws into the cylinder. The handle gives a secure grip and provides easier handling compared to a traditional fire extinguisher. The large trigger is easy to use even for people with limited strength in their hands and the safety pin prevents the extinguisher from being triggered unintentionally.

FINAL DESIGN

Make It Fit into the Family

The new extinguisher comes in three colour combinations. The red and white of the original Firephant and a new version in brushed aluminum, giving the extinguisher a more rugged expression. The handle stays black for all versions to reduce manufacturing cost and avoid difficult colour matching between the cylinder and handle.

 

To make the new extinguisher fit into Firephant’s product line certain elements were kept from the original. One example of this is the characteristic rounded shape in the bottom of the original cylinder. Another is the use of a similar safety pin and pressure gauge.

FINAL DESIGN

Hold Me Tight and Don't Let Go

Equally important as the extinguisher itself was where and how to mount it in the car.  We wanted a solution that would cause no permanent damage to the car, meaning no drilling. After evaluation of 12 different placements both inside the cabin and in the trunk the most universal and safest solution was to mount it in the trunk of the car. This also makes sure that you don’t stay inside the burning car looking for the fire extinguisher.

 

The final solution for the holder is a simple solution made of bent steel wire. This makes it easy and cheap to produce as it requires little material. On the back of the holder is a Velcro developed specifically for the felt fabric found in most cars. To really secure the extinguisher the holder has a tether that connects to the loading loops in the trunk.
