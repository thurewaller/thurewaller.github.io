Telling Stories from Exchange Students in Asia

Asia Magazine is a biannual magazine featuring stories from Chalmers students on exchange in Asia. The stories are written by students living in different cities around East Asia. The topics of the articles range from the marriage market in Shanghai to the weirdest findings at 7Eleven in Taiwan. The aim of the magazine is to inform and inspire students and staff at Chalmers University of Technology about what it’s like to study in Asia.

 

The staff of the magazine consist of an Art Director and an Editor in Chief who are exchange students based in Taiwan. When I joined as Art Director I wanted to refresh the magazine which had looked similar for a few years. During my exchange year I worked on the magazine beside my full time master studies in Industrial Design.

FORMAT

New Size and New Look

One of the first things I did was to change the size of the magazine. The magazine had previously had a large format suitable for beautiful photos. But with content providers being engineering students – not professional photographers – the photos received were of varying quality. To get around this problem I reduced the size of the magazine giving the text more attention. I also replaced the glossy paper with an uncoated heavier paper giving the magazine a more premium and tactile feel.

LAYOUT

Find the Articles for You

The aim of the magazine is to inform and inspire other students about exchange studies. To make it easier for the reader to find the articles of interest, each spread has the title and country of origin in the header.

DESIGN

Creating a Common Thread

The previous issues of the magazine were too clattered, using too many typefaces and colour combinations. My approach was to streamline the magazine and create a common base for all articles. This created a consistency through out the magazine. Each article was then complemented with its unique illustrations and photos to make it stand out.
