We All Need a HearO

Untreated hearing loss is a major health concern leading to social isolation, depression and dementia. In Sweden 19% of the population suffers from some kind of hearing loss but only 25% of the affected treat their hearing loss. In this master thesis I and Lukas Riedel set out to explore how to get more people to treat their hearing loss.

 

From interviews with audiologists and people with hearing loss we found that the largest barrier to getting hearing aids today is stigma. The stigma surrounding hearing loss and hearing aids make people feel old, stupid and handicapped.

 

Eyewear is a product category that once suffered a similar stigma as hearing aids. Today though glasses are a way to express style and fashion. Would it be possible to take hearing aids on a similar journey? Keep scrolling to find out!

PRE-STUDY

A Visit to the Audiologist

To get hearing aids you go to an audiologist, so that’s what we did. A visit to the audiologist is like going to the hospital in many ways. You feel sick even though your fit as a fiddle. At the audiologists we observed the steps you go through when getting hearing aids and talked about peoples’ feelings towards hearing aids.

PRE-STUDY

Talking to People

Why aren’t people using hearing aids even though they clearly need them? To find out we had to find the right people and ask the right questions. The main reason we found is the stigma surrounding hearing loss and hearing aids. Users don’t want to feel old, stupid and handicapped.

“When I go to the disco to hit on girls I can't wear hearing aids. I want to be perceived as a healthy man rather than elderly.”

Hearing Aid Refuser

PRE-STUDY

Telling a Story

Design is storytelling, therefore we created a storyboard meant to communicate the barriers people experience when getting hearing aids. The storyboard is based on customer journey maps we did with hearing aid users. It shows that the hardest part is before you actually start using the hearing aids.

IDEATION & PROTOTYPING

Tackling the Stigma

Hearing aids are associated with so much negativity that it would be almost impossible to create an updated version that doesn’t suffer from the stigma. A new radical solution is needed. A product that blurs the line between people with and without hearing loss. A product everybody wants to own and use.

IDEATION & PROTOTYPING

Finding the Function Everybody Wants

Okay so we just need to make a product that everybody wants, no problem! Or? How do you find the function everybody wants? After a lot of probing with users in interviews, surveys and co-creation sessions we found that everybody have situations where they want a better sound experience. From the crowded bar to the open office space, there is a need to control the soundscape.

FINAL DESIGN

Introducing a HearO

From hard work, empathy and passion a new HearO was born. This HearO has three strategies to fight the hearing aid stigma: style, easy access and functions everybody wants. The key functions are sound control and increased wellbeing.

FINAL DESIGN

Pick Your Style

Casual, formal, high fashion or no fashion. The HearO is for everyone. With the interchangeable discs the appearance of the HearO can be changed a lot. There’s a disc for every style and every occasion.

FINAL DESIGN

There’s a New Brand in Town

To get away from hearing aids we created a new brand for the HearO. The visual identity builds around a dynamic logo using the different discs of the product. To distance the brand and product further from the hearing aid industry we created a HearO brand store. Here the customers (not patients as in hearing aid clinics) can get help scanning their ears for the perfect fit and find the discs suiting their style. The stores are located in city centres making it easy to pop in on a lunch break.

FINAL DESIGN

You Are in Control

The HearO lets you control your soundscape. Music is controlled using the right HearO-bud. Just turn the disc to change the volume of the music playing. Turn the disc on the left HearO-bud to control the volume of the surrounding soundscape. The HearO-buds can help you filter out the sounds you want to hear from the noise you don’t. This is possible thanks to EEG-sensors measuring brain activity and multiple microphones using beamforming technology.

 

For even better sound filtering the HearO-buds come with a portable charging case featuring nine microphones. Placed on a table in a noisy bar or restaurant the case helps filter out sounds in all directions.

FINAL DESIGN

Your Own Life Coach in the App

The ear is a great place to measure things like heart rate and fitness level. Much better than the wrist or chest used by many fitness tracking devices today. This means that the HearO can keep track of your physical progress. This is done in the HearO-app. But that’s not all. With the biometric sensor in the HearO it’s possible to measure mental load as well. From the HearO app, sound therapy can help you relax or stay focused.
