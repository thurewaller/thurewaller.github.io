Giving SSRS an Eye in the Sky

The Swedish Sea Rescue Society (SSRS) is responsible for 70 percent of all sea rescue missions in Sweden. This without any government funding. SSRS is financed by membership fees, donations and voluntary work.

 

For this bachelor thesis, SSRS wanted to explore the possibilities of using drones in their rescue missions. They had found a fixed wing drone matching their needs in terms of speed and range. Our mission was to find a way store the drone when not in use and a way to launch it autonomously – a drone launching box.

PRE-STUDY

Learning From Sea Rescuers

To understand what it’s like to work as a sea rescuer we visited sea rescue stations. At the stations we interviewed the people working there and also had the opportunity to go with them on a mission. This was important for us to be able to integrate drones into their workflow.

PRE-STUDY

Where Should We Place It?

A drone with a wingspan of 2.3 meters means the drone launching box can’t be placed anywhere. There has to be enough space for it to safely launch the drone. At the same time it can’t be too far away from the sea rescue station as a sea rescuer needs to replace the drone after a mission. As SSRS is dependent on donations the drone launching box should preferably work as an advertisement. To find placement options we visited a number of harbours and other places around the West Coast of Sweden.

IDEATION

The Creation of Three Concepts

The ideation phase of the project resulted in three concepts that were presented to the client. The three concepts shown below all use a ramp to launch the drone. More unconventional launching mechanism were investigated but seen as infeasible at the time of this project. All the concepts can rotate 360 degrees to catch sufficient wind at launch.

FINAL CONCEPT

Make It Fly

After evaluation of the three concepts we choose to develop the second concept further into a final concept. The main reasons for that was that it took up the least ground space and worked best as an advertisement. The final concept uses a ramp with a pneumatic launching mechanism, sufficient to launch the drone even in strong winds.
